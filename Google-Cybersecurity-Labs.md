# 🛡️ Botium Toys Security Audit

## Overview
This project presents a basic internal security audit for Botium Toys. The goal is to evaluate current security controls and assess compliance with industry standards such as PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC.

---

## Controls Checklist

- Least Privilege: ❌ No  
- Disaster Recovery Plan: ❌ No  
- Password Policy: ❌ No  
- Separation of Duties: ❌ No  
- Firewall: ✅ Yes  
- Intrusion Detection System (IDS): ❌ No  
- Backups: ❌ No  
- Antivirus Software: ✅ Yes  
- Manual monitoring (legacy systems): ⚠️ Partial  
- Encryption: ❌ No  
- Password Management System: ❌ No  
- Physical Locks: ✅ Yes  
- CCTV Surveillance: ✅ Yes  
- Fire Detection Systems: ✅ Yes  

---

## Compliance Checklist

### PCI DSS
- Access restricted to authorized users: ❌ No  
- Secure storage of credit card data: ❌ No  
- Encryption implemented: ❌ No  
- Strong password management policies: ❌ No  

---

### GDPR
- Data is secured and private: ❌ No  
- Breach notification within 72 hours: ✅ Yes  
- Data classification and inventory: ❌ No  
- Privacy policies enforced: ✅ Yes  

---

### SOC (System and Organization Controls)
- User access policies: ❌ No  
- Sensitive data confidentiality: ❌ No  
- Data integrity: ✅ Yes  
- Data access limited to authorized users: ❌ No  

---

## Recommendation
Botium Toys should implement critical security controls including least privilege access, encryption, disaster recovery planning, intrusion detection systems, and proper password management. Additionally, improving compliance requires better access control, data classification, and protection of sensitive information.
